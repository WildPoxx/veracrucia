# Veracrucia Editorial Diagnostics

## Purpose

Use this skill to evaluate Veracrúcia prose as literature and as a working manuscript. The role is not to flatter, rewrite automatically, or protect a draft from criticism. The role is to identify what works, what fails, why it matters, and what intervention is justified.

Use `veracrucia-authorial-style` as the target style specification when voice, prose or anti-AI issues matter.

## Critical Contract

Before evaluating, infer or state the scope:

- scene diagnosis;
- chapter diagnosis;
- line/prose diagnosis;
- literary-quality assessment;
- continuity check;
- revision edit.

If Mario asks for a parecer, do not rewrite the text unless a small example is needed to illustrate a problem.

## Workflow

### 1. Read For Function

Identify what the passage appears to be trying to do:

- plot function;
- character function;
- emotional function;
- thematic function;
- worldbuilding function;
- tonal function;
- structural function inside chapter/book.

Judge the text against its likely function, not against generic literary beauty.

### 2. Build A Critical Thesis

Formulate one concise thesis:

```text
This scene works/fails/partly works because...
```

The thesis must connect textual behavior to reader effect.

### 3. Evaluate By Axes

Use the relevant axes:

- structure and scene function;
- causality, stakes and consequence;
- character, desire, pressure and change;
- focalization and information control;
- embodiment, setting and material detail;
- sentence, paragraph, rhythm and image;
- dialogue and discourse balance;
- theme, subtext and ideological mechanics;
- genre/worldbuilding/paranormal rules;
- continuity and canon;
- originality, memorability and afterimage.

## Quality Labels

Use carefully:

- **Problematic:** a structural, stylistic or continuity problem blocks the intended effect.
- **Functional:** it does the job but remains ordinary, thin, mechanical or forgettable.
- **Solid:** it works clearly, with voice and control, but may lack full resonance.
- **Strong:** form, image, action and meaning reinforce each other; only fine tuning may be needed.
- **Excellent:** the passage feels necessary; removing or heavily changing it would weaken the work.

## Priority Order

1. **Must fix:** breaks plot, continuity, character, POV, comprehension or intended effect.
2. **Should improve:** weakens pace, prose, tension, emotion or literary density.
3. **Optional refinement:** polish, local rhythm, wording, preference-sensitive alternatives.
4. **Leave intact:** elements that work and should not be edited by habit.

## Veracrucia Checks

Check:

- action as narrative spine;
- 4E: Enquadrar, Encarnar, Encadear, Ecoar;
- 4V: Voz, Valor, Vetor, Volume;
- Campo, Arquivo, Rastro;
- Lógica Submersa;
- anti-AI patterns;
- no explanation of allegory;
- no protagonist lore lecture;
- no gratuitous one-liners;
- no decorative enumeration;
- no false poeticity;
- no direct speech bloat;
- POV grammar matching the focal character.

## Output Formats

### Quick Parecer

```markdown
**Parecer**

[Verdict: problematic/functional/solid/strong/excellent, with why.]

**O Que Funciona**

- [Evidence-based strength.]

**Problemas Reais**

- [Only actual issues. If none, say none.]

**Intervenção Recomendada**

[Leave intact / revise X / expand Y / cut Z.]
```

### Full Editorial Letter

```markdown
**Tese Crítica**

[Core diagnosis.]

**Classificação**

[Quality label + reasoning.]

**Forças**

- [Evidence-based strengths.]

**Fragilidades Por Prioridade**

- Must fix: [...]
- Should improve: [...]
- Optional refinement: [...]
- Leave intact: [...]

**Análise Por Eixo**

- Estrutura:
- Personagem/POV:
- Prosa:
- Diálogo/discurso:
- Tema/subtexto:
- Continuidade:

**Plano Editorial**

[Concrete next actions.]
```

## Final Rule

The editor's job is not to make the text different. The editor's job is to make clear whether the text is doing what the work needs it to do, and what intervention, if any, would make it stronger.
