# Veracrucia Authorial Style

## Purpose

Use this skill when drafting, rewriting, expanding, revising, or diagnosing prose for **Veracrúcia**. The goal is not to imitate a fixed formula, but to keep generated text close to Mario Bastos's authorial direction and away from generic AI prose.

Mario is the author and creative director. Do not invent canon, character past, plot resolution, or aesthetic doctrine without basis.

## Style Routing

Before drafting or revising, identify:

1. project context;
2. POV or narrative camera;
3. scene function;
4. dominant pressure: body, work, institution, archive, city, document, route, perception, model, memory, fear, class;
5. what the reader must know;
6. what must stay submerged;
7. active reference mechanism, if any.

Core style prevails over reference authors. Veracrúcia rules prevail over generic dark urban fantasy. POV matrix prevails over interchangeable clever narration.

## Core Principles

- Concrete action is the narrative spine.
- Establish where the scene unfolds, who acts, what pressures them, what objects and systems shape the moment, and what changes.
- Show before telling.
- Philosophy must arise from scene, body, object, work, guilt, investigation, institution or city.
- Preserve ambiguity, subtext and tension.
- Avoid empty lyricism, generic profundity and explanatory allegory.

Use the 4E rule:

- **Enquadrar:** locate the prose camera and circumstance.
- **Encarnar:** embody information in matter.
- **Encadear:** chain action, reaction and consequence.
- **Ecoar:** allow reflection or poetry only after the scene earns it.

Use the 4V rule for discourse:

- **Voz:** direct speech when voice matters.
- **Valor:** direct speech when the line changes pressure.
- **Vetor:** indirect narration when information matters more than exact words.
- **Volume:** control how much speech the scene can bear.

## POV Tendencies

- **Henrique:** ironic, tired, defensive, guilty, intellectually evasive; never a lore explainer.
- **Miguel:** dry, observant, ethical, vain, institutionally tense; investigation as moral irritation and professional pride.
- **Jackie:** body, speed, asphalt, app, hunger, rage, grief, humor; politics through work and risk, not lecture.
- **Augusto:** data, script, monitor, lab, protocol, model, variable; corporate horror through method and measurement.
- **Dona Jandira:** coffee, cigarette, money, gossip, memory, social reading; practical cynicism with affection.
- **Eddie:** performance, metrics, app interface, slogan, optimization; violence translated into marketable cheer.

## Weird, Archive and Allegory

For paranormal, institutional, historical or allegorical material:

- use **Campo, Arquivo, Rastro** before revelation;
- show the social field that normalizes or misreads the strange;
- let records, reports, servers, laudos, files, rumors and institutions act as material power;
- define internal rules offstage, but expose them through partial clues, costs, symptoms, failures and contradictions;
- do not explain allegory as allegory.

## Anti-AI Pass

Before delivering prose, remove or repair:

- formulaic profundity, especially "not merely X, but Y" patterns;
- explanations of symbols;
- generic phrases such as "é importante destacar", "vale ressaltar", "nesse sentido", "em suma", "jornada", "camadas de significado";
- poetic sentences that do not carry scene function;
- gratuitous isolated one-line paragraphs;
- article before proper names in narration;
- decorative enumerations that replace observed matter;
- direct speech that only transfers information;
- protagonist explanations that expose the planning scaffold.

## Veracrucia Text Conventions

- Use `Henrique`, `Miguel`, `Jackie`, `Augusto`, not `o Henrique`, `o Miguel`, `a Jackie`, `o Augusto`, unless deliberate character speech requires it.
- Use curly quotation marks for spoken dialogue in final prose.
- Use italics sparingly for internal thought.
- Use spaced hyphen ` - ` for incidental insertions when needed.
- Avoid long em dash in narrative prose.
- Use `Eidolon` / `Eidolons`.
- Use `Corporação Axsoma` as the Portuguese canonical name unless quoting a foreign in-world document.

## Style Tests

Ask before finalizing:

1. Did the passage grow from concrete matter or abstract diagnosis?
2. Could it belong to another urban fantasy if names were swapped?
3. Did narration explain what the reader should infer?
4. Does the city feel specific through weather, labor, institution, class, transit, police, university, church, coast, app, iron or salt?
5. Does each poetic sentence communicate mood, body, environment, action, memory, relationship, perception or tension?
6. Did the scene follow Enquadrar, Encarnar, Encadear, Ecoar?
7. Did speech follow Voz, Valor, Vetor, Volume?
