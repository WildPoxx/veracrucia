# Veracrucia Scene Planner

## Purpose

Use this skill to plan Veracrúcia scenes through a practical questionnaire before drafting prose.

Do not start drafting unless Mario explicitly asks. Accept incomplete answers and mark unknowns as `Em aberto`.

## Interaction Rules

1. Ask small batches of 3 to 6 questions.
2. Prefer practical questions over theory.
3. After each answer, extract fixed decisions and ambiguities.
4. If alternatives exist, compare consequences before choosing.
5. Do not force every field to be filled.
6. Preserve anti-AI and style rules from `veracrucia-authorial-style`.

## Workflow

### 1. Establish Scene Identity

Ask or infer:

- chapter and scene number;
- POV or narrative camera;
- working title;
- current file/path, if any;
- whether this is plan only, rewrite plan or preparation for drafting.

### 2. Questionnaire Blocks

Use this order as a menu:

1. identity and dramatic function;
2. required plot events and forbidden revelations;
3. setting, bodies, objects and sensory anchors;
4. conflict, turn and ending image;
5. tone, POV, irony and anti-AI risks;
6. open decisions and alternatives.

### 3. Synthesize

After each batch, briefly state:

- decisions fixed;
- ambiguities still open;
- structural risks noticed.

### 4. Produce The Plan

When enough material exists, produce a Markdown plan with:

- Identificação;
- Função da cena;
- Promessa, progresso e payoff;
- Eventos obrigatórios;
- Informação: revelar, insinuar, esconder;
- Conflito e virada;
- Cenário e materialidade;
- Movimento interno do POV;
- Tom e voz;
- Imagem de entrada;
- Imagem de saída;
- Riscos e proibições;
- Perguntas em aberto;
- Sinopse operacional.

## File Naming

When Mario asks to save a plan inside a chapter folder, use:

- `Planos/00. Planejamento Estrutural - Capitulo X.md`
- `Planos/01. Plano - [Personagem] - [Titulo da Cena].md`
- `Planos/02. Plano - [Personagem] - [Titulo da Cena].md`
- `Planos/03. Plano - [Personagem] - [Titulo da Cena].md`
- `Planos/04. Plano - [Personagem ou Extra] - [Titulo da Cena].md`

Keep prose in `Cenas/`, planning material in `Planos/`, and old drafts/previews in `Versoes/`.

## Quality Tests

Before finalizing a plan, check:

1. Does the scene change something?
2. Is the function in the chapter or act clear?
3. Does the POV know too much?
4. Does the reader learn too much too early?
5. Are objects, bodies and setting concrete enough?
6. Is there a turn, even if subtle?
7. Is the ending image strong but not falsely profound?
8. Does the plan protect against generic AI prose?
